It is possible (but unlikely) that someone malicious could break into our systems and access your data. It is also possible that we could make a mistake in how we release the data, allowing someone else to figure out your real identity in the data set. We will do our best to prevent these outcomes, but these are always risks with digital information. No security and privacy protections are perfect.

Batea is designed from the ground up to minimize the amount of data that it collects and to protect what data it does collect from simple disclosure. However, it is impossible to predict how even a portion of your browsing patterns might be used against you in the future, so it is difficult to assess how great of a risk this is.

However, many other organizations collect browsing patterns, without making any comittment to protecting your privacy. DocGraph (the researching organization behind Batea) is likely among the least risky organizations that have an interest in your browsing patterns.
